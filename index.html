Numbers - Fact or Lie ?

At first glance, numbers are able to portray factual information to us such as the cost of our electrical bill, how many days are there in a year and so on.

However, numbers can also be misleading!

For example...

Based on the bar chart, we can conclude that Maharashtra has the highest number of COVID-19 deaths as of 21st September 2021.

Indirectly, these numbers imply that Maharashtra is the most dangerous place to be in India. However, we're not looking at the full context of the situation.

In reality...

The primary variable we should be wary about while looking at the number of COVID-19 deaths is it's death ratio (%).

In terms of death ratio, Maharashtra is actually the 2nd highest (2.12%), only losing out to Punjab (2.74%).

Therefore, the most dangerous state in India is Punjab since the fatality rate due to COVID-19 there is the highest.

Why stop there...

Using an entirely different visualisation idiom such as a choropleth map can provide information more effectively.

Or alternatively...

The rose chart is an effective visualiation idiom too.

In conclusion, numbers can truthfully mislead others without proper context to the situation.
