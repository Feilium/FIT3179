Numbers - Fact or Misleading?

At a glance, numbers are values used to represent the quantity of objects in our daily lives. Numbers are able to portray factual information to us such as the cost of our electrical bill, how many days are there in a week and so on. However, numbers can also be misleading!

For an example...

Two bar charts consisting of the top 10 Indian State/Union Territory with the highest and lowest COVID-19 Deaths (as of 21st September 2021) has been computed

Through these charts, we are able to arrive at the conclusion that 'Maharashtra' has the highest number of COVID-19 deaths, while the union territory of Dadra, Nagar Haveli, Daman and Diu has the lowest number of deaths.

Indirectly, these numbers imply that Maharashtra is the most dangerous place to be in India. However, we're not looking at the most important aspect of these numbers, the context it is placed in.

When in reality...

The primary variable we should be wary about while looking at the number of COVID-19 deaths is it's death ratio.

The greater the death ratio ( Number of Deaths due to COVID-19 over Total Number of Confirmed COVID-19 Cases), the greater the likelihood that the state/union territory lacks medical facilities.

Therefore, states/union terriotries with high death ratios is what we, as an audience have to pay attention towards to since numbers can be factual, but misleading given the context of situation.

Why stop there...

Using an entirely different visualisation idiom such as a choropleth map can provide information more efficiently.
